Shattered Bytes.
Recovery is not enough.

A six-act browser game that turns manual data carving into an investigation. Players recover raw bytes, but they are evaluated on whether the method-and the conclusion-can be defended.

RoleDesigner & developer
Duration10–15 minutes
StackReact, Vite, Tailwind
Format6-act serious game
01 / The design problem

Teach forensic reasoning,
not pattern matching.

Manual data carving is often reduced to finding a file header and extracting everything up to a footer. That creates the wrong mental model. A valid signature may be a decoy; a file can be fragmented; recoverable evidence can be irrelevant; and an incomplete artefact remains useful only when its limits are reported honestly.

I designed Shattered Bytes around a stricter premise: signatures generate hypotheses, not conclusions. The player works as a junior analyst on a fictional ransomware case, Operation Shattered Meridian, and every technical action must answer an investigative question.

02 / Campaign

One new complication
at a time.

The campaign uses progressive scaffolding. Every act preserves earlier skills and introduces one new source of uncertainty, so the final challenge becomes a synthesis rather than an unrelated puzzle.

Act 01False lead

Recognize exact PNG boundaries and reject a plausible decoy.

Act 02Fragmentation

Reassemble two byte runs without including unrelated gap residue.

Act 03Relevance

Choose the recoverable artefact that actually answers the case question.

Act 04Partition logic

Parse little-endian MBR data and derive the correct LBA byte offset.

Act 05Uncertainty

Decode partial XOR-obfuscated evidence and avoid overclaiming recovery.

Act 06Synthesis

Combine fragmentation, decoy rejection, XOR recovery, and incident relevance.

Before analysis, an evidence-intake interaction introduces the sealed drive, write blocker, workstation, and hash verification. After the final recovery, players arrange six artefacts into a coherent incident chain-because evidence only becomes useful when it supports a defensible reconstruction.

03 / Assessment

The game scores
how you think.

Completion alone hides the difference between disciplined analysis and lucky extraction. The game records search commands, navigation, byte selections, fragment stashing, compose attempts, XOR operations, report claims, hint use, and procedural decisions.

Those actions feed a score and an eight-dimensional mastery profile: signature recognition, offset precision, fragment reconstruction, trial-and-error discipline, obfuscation handling, procedure discipline, forensic reasoning, and reporting quality.

Assessment for learning

Wrong actions are allowed rather than silently blocked. Broad selections, premature carving, and unsupported reports remain visible in the investigation timeline and affect the final feedback. The mistake becomes part of the lesson.

04 / Implementation

Deterministic by design.

Shattered Bytes is a static React/Vite application with no backend. Source assets are transformed into deterministic JSON level dumps; explicit metadata defines genuine evidence regions, decoys, fragments, and expected reporting outcomes.

This makes validation explainable and classroom sessions repeatable. The system can distinguish an exact fragment from an overlapping range that includes residue without relying on hidden AI or an opaque server decision. GitHub Actions deploys the game as a public, installation-free demo.

05 / Boundaries

A training environment,
not a forensic suite.

The dumps are synthetic, the chain-of-custody interaction is simplified, and single-byte XOR introduces obfuscation rather than modeling modern encryption. The score is educational feedback, not professional certification.

Those constraints are deliberate: they keep the experience short, inspectable, and suitable for comparing methods in a laboratory debrief. The next meaningful validation step is classroom testing-observing not just whether students enjoy the game, but whether they can explain why their recovery is defensible.
